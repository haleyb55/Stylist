## The vibe

She goes for *designed* clothes — pieces where someone has clearly made specific decisions about texture, hardware, drape, or proportion. Surface intervention is the entry point: ruffles, smocking, studs, paillettes, embossing, contrast topstitching, applied appliqué, drape that's been thought about. The silhouette can be soft or tough, prairie or moto, but the piece always has a *gesture* — an asymmetric drape, a defined waist, a sleeve that does something, a print that's making a point. She's drawn to 70s romantic-prairie and 90s/Y2K Italian archive in roughly equal measure, and she mixes registers in one piece — feminine fabric with tough hardware, ruffled cuffs on a bomber. She's not minimalist and not maximalist. She's a feature-collector with a strong nose for designer pieces that haven't been ruined by trying to be flattering.

Brand reference points: Paloma Wool's playful proportion, vintage Miu Miu's archival weirdness, All Saints' soft-tough mix, Comme des Garçons' conceptual sensibility.

## What makes something for Haley (in priority order)

These are the features that predict her saves. An item doesn't need every feature — strong hits on 2-3 plus vibe-match is enough. An item with no surface intervention, no waist play, and no mixed-register tension is probably not for her even if the brand is right.

### 1. Surface intervention as the design event
Applied or built-in detail is what makes a piece interesting to her. Smocking, ruffles, ties, knots, paillettes, grommets, studs, contrast topstitching, lace insets, ruched panels, asymmetric drapes, embossed leather, toggle hardware, peplums, **crinkle/pleated/textural fabric, ruching, surface texture as the design event itself**. Plain pieces with no surface or structural intervention are rarely for her.

### 2. Interesting waist
High-waist, defined, contrast-banded, drop waist, peplum, ruched, corseted, lace-up, belted. Any deliberate waistline treatment scores. Shapeless or no-waist silhouettes rarely work.

### 3. Mixed registers
Feminine fabric + tough hardware. Soft drape + structured belt. Prairie sleeve + grommet trim. The tension between two registers in one piece is the point.

### 4. Balloon / puff sleeves
One of the strongest single-feature signals. Confirmed across 6+ saves.

### 4b. Asymmetric one-shoulder / off-shoulder draping
Recurring signature across multiple saves (Donna Karan, Ganni, Max Mara, T by Alexander Wang). One-bare-shoulder satin drapes, asymmetric panel falls, knotted single-shoulder details. Often in black silk or satin.

### 5. Interesting over flattering
Sheer, gathered, slightly-off, conceptual, vintage-weird all welcomed. Bias against "slimming" or "polished" framing.

### 6. Vintage / archival
Explicit "Vintage 200X" or designer archive listings score a boost. Repeat positive signal across Missoni, Mizrahi, Miu Miu, Balenciaga.

## Soft negatives and the override principle

Some silhouettes and cuts are normally a no, but **exceptional surface intervention can override them**. The features above are stronger signals than these soft negatives.

### Soft negatives
- Skinny-leg trousers and skinny denim — normally a no.
- Cropped/ankle-length trousers — normally a no, especially in combination with skinny.
- Plain pieces with no surface or structural detail (regardless of cut).
- Unintentionally shapeless / no-waist silhouettes — sack dresses, plain shifts, etc. **Deliberately shapeless silhouettes are not a negative: kaftans, ponchos, capes, oversized-by-design blouses, drape-forward pieces are all fine when the shapelessness is the point.**
- "Flattering"-coded pieces (bodycon, slim-everything) without conceptual interest.

### Override examples
- Dsquared² skinny cropped jeans with heavy floral appliqué and sequin surface — saved despite the cut, because the surface intervention is so strong.

The scoring logic should treat soft negatives as a *deduction* on the score, not a filter. A piece with strong surface intervention (feature 1) and waist play (feature 2) can still surface even with a soft-negative cut, but the bar is higher.

## Sub-aesthetics

Any can apply, often multiple in one piece.

- **Romantic prairie / 70s** — peasant necks, balloon sleeves, prairie collars, ruffles, ties.
- **Western / Americana** — pointed leather, Western yokes, embossed leather, stacked wood heels, turquoise piping, Western shirt details.
- **Workwear / utility** — cargo pockets, utility jackets, toggle clasps, drawstring waists, D-ring belts, trench coats.
- **90s / Y2K archival designer** — vintage Miu Miu, Balenciaga, Givenchy, Moschino Couture, Isaac Mizrahi era. Italian and European archives especially.
- **Body-skimming long-line knit** — fitted maxi knits, striped or marled, often with personality (print, texture, hardware).

## Price posture

**Saves above $100 are aspirational, not buy-intent.** She saves things she loves but only actually buys under $100 unless a piece is truly rare.

| Range | Posture | Surfacing |
|---|---|---|
| Under $50 | Buy-zone, easy yes | High frequency |
| $50–100 | Buy-zone sweet spot | High frequency |
| $100–200 | Save-zone, strong taste fit only | Lower frequency — still surface but bias the feed toward sub-$100 |
| $200+ | Save-zone, exceptional only | Rare — only when score is 9+ |

The feed should bias toward buy-zone so the things she might actually buy don't get buried under aspirational saves. Treat surface-this and might-actually-buy as the same action; the price tier determines how aggressive the surfacing is.

## Scoring guidance for Claude

When scoring an item against this profile, return:
- `taste_score` (0-10) — fit against the features and vibe above, ignoring price.
- `reasoning` — 2-3 sentences referencing specific profile elements. Be honest about uncertainty.
- `features_hit` — array of which priority features apply (1-6).
- `sub_aesthetics` — array of which sub-aesthetics fit.
- `flags` — concerns (e.g. 'wrong size', 'condition Fair', 'plain piece with no surface intervention', 'too flattering-coded').

Bias toward "interesting over flattering." If something is conceptually strong but unconventional, lean higher, not lower.

The price multiplier and surfacing threshold are applied in code, not in the scoring prompt itself — keep taste scoring and price posture separate.
